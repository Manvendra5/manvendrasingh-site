Far Cry 3: Blood Dragon review

September 15, 2021

Rating : 9/10

Far Cry 3: Blood Dragon Blood Dragon is an attempt by Ubisoft to release an expansion as a stand alone game, coming in at a low price point.

The order of the day is a different mix of action to what we’ve come to expect from the Far Cry series, and a complete 180 from the original Far Cry 3 release.

Have Ubisoft managed to fit enough content and enjoyment into the mix? Read on to find out!

The Review

In Blood Dragon, the year is 2007, and the world is suffering the aftermath of a nuclear war. Technology and man has become one in the same, with the U.S army creating cyborg super soldiers. You play as Rex, an American cybernetic super-soldier, sent in with another operative and friend, Spider, to investigate Colonel Sloan, an elite agent who has gone rogue, stopping the launch of a nuclear missile in the process.

Far Cry 3: Blood Dragon is designed with a very retro style look to it. Everything is full of bright colors despite being set in a destroyed wasteland, and is accompanied by a very fitting retro musical score. The game takes quite a few references from iconic movies and shows from the eighties, the most notable example being the Terminator.

Vehicles exist in the game although they are few and far between. There are basically three in the game: a jeep (with or without a mounted gun), a boat, and a hang-glider. They will respawn upon death or game loading, and the cyber soldiers wil occasionally appear randomly driving either a boat or a jeep.

Far Cry 3: Blood Dragon plays like any basic open world game, and if you have ever played Far Cry 2 or Far Cry 3, the game is very similar in style, yet not in substance. The game has barely anything to do in its world.

You have two types of side missions, rescue and hunting, but they are always the same, but with more or less enemies and at different locations. Completing these missions, however, unlock modifications for the weapons you find throughout the game, allowing them to become more powerful and/or useful as you progress. There are collectibles in the game, such as finding VHS tapes and old televisions, but they feel tacked on and boring.

While playing the game, the games soundtrack really stood out the most, full of upbeat pop and retro music that somehow managed to fit the worlds theme. The games difficulty is fairly balanced, i found myself dying a few times, but never really being overwhelmed, especially being able to recover health most of the game, even without needing any injections, a balance breaker I have hated since Far Cry 2.

System Benchmark

As with all PC games, I strongly advise following the recommended system specs to ensure a smooth and enjoyable game.

  • OS: Windows® XP (SP3) / Windows Vista® (SP2) / Windows® 7 (SP1) / Windows® 8
  • Processor: 2.93 GHz Intel® Core™ i3–530 or 3.10 GHz AMD Phenom™ II X2 550 or better
  • Memory: 4 GB
  • Graphics: 1024 MB DirectX 11–complaint or higher
  • DirectX®: DirectX® 11
  • Hard Drive: 3 GB HD space
  • Requires a UPlay account.

The Verdict

Fans of Far Cry 2 and Far Cry 3, as well as those who love retro culture in their video games, will enjoy Blood Dragon. Coming in at around $20AU, it’s debatable if the price is worth it, as the content is rather lacking, but the incredible sound track, voice work and the fact it remains a stand alone expansion almost justifies the price.
